Hardware Phase — Upcoming: The RL policy and software architecture are complete. The hardware stack below has been fully specced and costed — physical build and integration is the team's next milestone.

Planned Component Breakdown

Compute — Raspberry Pi 4

The planned onboard brain. Will run ROS 2 Humble, the PPO inference loop at 15 Hz, and the Safety Guard Node simultaneously on 4 GB LPDDR4 RAM.

  • Quad-core Cortex-A72 @ 1.8 GHz
  • 4 GB LPDDR4 RAM
  • USB 3.0 for peripherals & camera
  • GPIO for I²C sensor bus
Primary Compute Specced

Actuation — Differential Drive

Two independently driven wheels will enable zero-radius turns and precise odometric tracking — critical for tight warehouse aisle navigation.

  • L298N dual H-bridge motor driver
  • PWM speed control per wheel
  • Quadrature wheel encoders (odometry)
  • 5 V / 3 A regulated power rail
PWM Control Encoder Feedback

Sensing — Perception Suite

Multi-modal sensor fusion will give the robot a complete picture of its immediate environment for both navigation and safety enforcement.

  • VL53L0X ToF sensors ×4 — ranging up to 2 m
  • MPU-6050 IMU — 6-axis roll/pitch/yaw
  • Sharp IR sensors ×2 — short-range wall-follow
  • HC-SR04 ultrasonic (redundancy fallback)
Sensor Fusion EKF Odometry

Vision — Pi Camera Module

Raw video capture for visual navigation pipelines. Planned to feed Tiny-YOLO for obstacle classification, with SLAM and visual odometry correction in future iterations.

  • Pi Camera Module 2 / 3
  • ESP32-CAM optional secondary angle
  • OpenCV lane & marker detection pipeline

Alternatives & Fallbacks

Ultrasonic Fallback

HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors can substitute IR units in cost-constrained builds, maintaining functional obstacle avoidance without the full perception suite.

Fallback Option

Edge Inference Upgrade

Policy inference can later migrate to a Coral USB Accelerator or TensorRT runtime — cutting latency below 20 ms and freeing CPU cycles for full camera processing.

Roadmap

IMU Upgrade Path

The MPU-6050 can be upgraded to a BNO055 for absolute orientation data and better heading stability — useful once real-world drift characteristics are measured.
