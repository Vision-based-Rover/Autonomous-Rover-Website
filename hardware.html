Bill of Materials — Physical Build
Every component was chosen to maximise performance per dollar. The full chassis, compute, sensing, and actuation stack costs a fraction of commercial AGV alternatives — and can be sourced, assembled, and replaced by any engineering team.

Component Breakdown

Compute — Raspberry Pi 4

The onboard brain. Runs ROS 2 Humble, the PPO inference loop at 15 Hz, and the Safety Guard Node simultaneously on 4 GB LPDDR4 RAM.

  • Quad-core Cortex-A72 @ 1.8 GHz
  • 4 GB LPDDR4 RAM
  • USB 3.0 for peripherals & camera
  • GPIO for I²C sensor bus
Primary Compute

Actuation — Differential Drive

Two independently driven wheels enable zero-radius turns and precise odometric tracking — critical for tight warehouse aisle navigation.

  • L298N dual H-bridge motor driver
  • PWM speed control per wheel
  • Quadrature wheel encoders (odometry)
  • 5 V / 3 A regulated power rail
PWM Control Encoder Feedback

Sensing — 360° Lidar Suite

Primary perception is now driven by a 360° Laser Range Scanner, providing high-frequency point clouds for SLAM and robust obstacle detection in dynamic environments.

  • RPLidar A1/A2 — 12m scanning radius
  • 8000+ samples per second
  • VL53L0X ToF Sensors — Redundant near-field coverage
  • MPU-6050 IMU — Fused for heading stability
Lidar SLAM Point Cloud

Vision — Pi Camera Module

Raw video capture for visual navigation pipelines. Currently feeds Tiny-YOLO for obstacle classification; future SLAM and visual odometry correction planned.

  • Pi Camera Module 2 / 3
  • ESP32-CAM optional secondary angle
  • OpenCV lane & marker detection pipeline

Redundancy & Fallbacks

Ultrasonic Fallback

HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensors substitute IR units in cost-constrained builds, maintaining functional obstacle avoidance without the full perception suite.
